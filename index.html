Research identity

Building mental health AI that can be evaluated before it is trusted.

Dr. Bazen Gashaw Teferra is an AI and mental health researcher in Toronto working across computer engineering, natural language processing, psychiatry, digital health, and equitable health service delivery. He is part of the AI for Mental Health research group. His work asks how language technologies can help identify and monitor mental health needs without replacing clinicians or weakening clinical accountability.

His research has moved from acoustic and linguistic signals of generalized anxiety disorder to transformer-based screening, LLM-based depression assessment, therapeutic communication evaluation, safety guardrails, agentic AI in psychiatry, and fairness auditing.
